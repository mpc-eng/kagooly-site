How It Works
From sofa to outside in 60 seconds

Open Kagooly. See what works near you right now. Go.

1

Open & discover

Your feed shows walks, runs, swims, rides and parkruns near you — each with a weather suitability score from 0 to 100. Sheltered forest walks score high on rainy days. Open moors don't.

2

Check the weather timeline

Every activity has an hour-by-hour weather breakdown. See exactly when rain stops, when wind picks up, and when the best window is. Not a binary forecast — a timeline you can plan around.

3

Go

Tap for facilities (parking, toilets, cafe, dog-friendly), directions to the start, and offline details for when you lose signal. Save your favourites. Build your list of go-to spots.

Features
Built for UK weather, UK data, UK prices

Every feature exists because a British person needed it on a Saturday morning.

Weather suitability score

Every activity gets a 0-100 score based on current conditions. Sheltered walks score high on rainy days. You'll never miss a good day again.

Hour-by-hour weather timeline

Not just "rain today." See exactly when it starts, stops, and what the window looks like. Plan around the weather, not because of it.

Family-friendly filters

Buggy-accessible, dog-friendly, toilets, cafe nearby, suitable for kids aged 5-12. The things that actually decide whether you go.

Offline when you need it

Saved activities work offline. Because UK mobile signal dies the moment you leave the car park. This is free, not premium.

Water quality ratings

Real-time Environment Agency bathing water data for 451 UK sites. Excellent, Good, Sufficient, or Poor. Know before you swim.

Travel time filter

15, 30, 45, or 60 minutes from your location. By car, public transport, or on foot. No point finding a walk that takes longer to reach than to walk.

It's a playful take on "cagoule" — the thin waterproof jacket every British person was made to pack by their mum. You wear a cagoule precisely because it might rain and you're going anyway. That's the whole philosophy of the app: whatever the weather, there's something to do outside.

UK-specific public data: Ordnance Survey open data for rights of way and footpaths, Environment Agency for bathing water quality, Sustrans for the National Cycle Network, parkrun for event locations, and weather data from the Met Office. We don't crowdsource routes from California — we use the UK's own infrastructure data.
Yes, deliberately. UK weather, UK rights of way, UK bathing water data, UK parkruns. We'd rather do one country properly than do the whole world badly. Three ways. First, weather awareness: every activity is ranked by how suitable it is given today's conditions. AllTrails ignores weather entirely. Third, UK data quality: AllTrails' UK trail data is notoriously poor — routes across private land, paths that follow A-roads. We use UK-specific datasets.
